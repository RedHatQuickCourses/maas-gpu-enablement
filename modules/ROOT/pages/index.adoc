= Models as a Service: Breaking the GPU Silos on OpenShift AI 3.4

Welcome to this *Red Hat OpenShift AI 3.4 - Models as a Service* QuickCourse! 

---

The CIO at ChocoTech Industries just called an emergency meeting.

"We’ve invested over $500,000 in GPU infrastructure across four teams," she says, reviewing the quarterly report. "Yet our R&D team is waiting three weeks for capacity while Quality Control’s GPUs sit idle 60% of the time. This doesn’t make sense."

ChocoTech needs to align their AI Strategy to optimize for GPU utilization across business units, moving to a centralized, governed platform where all teams can access AI models efficiently.

== The MaaS Mandate

Designed for Platform Engineers and Developers, this course provides the steps to build a governed Models as a Service (MaaS) ecosystem on OpenShift AI 3.4. We skip the trial-and-error of figuring out which operators and registries to install. Instead - you will learn how to immediately transform fragmented, isolated hardware into a fairly scheduled, multi-tenant shared service, empowering your business units to deploy agentic AI smoothly, securely, and at scale.

By the end of this course, you will be equipped to empower your lines of business with self-service models to build agentic AI applications smoothly and securely.

====
**Experience Level**: Intermediate
====

== Prerequisites

To get the most out of this QuickCourse, you should come to the table with:

* Red Hat OpenShift administration experience (version 4.20 or later)
* Basic Kubernetes operator management knowledge
* Familiarity with core GPU concepts and generic GPU enablement
* An understanding of API concepts and REST principles
* Basic command-line experience
* Networking - Routing traffic via Gateway API to the Mass Endpoint. 

== What You'll Learn

In this hands-on QuickCourse, you will:

* **MaaS Strategy**: Understand how Models as a Service solves complex GPU resource allocation challenges.
* **Architect a Hub**: Design and implement a centralized model hub featuring governed, token-based consumption.
* **Configure and Deploy**: Set up MaaS on OpenShift AI using operators and CRDs, and deploy generative AI models (using llm-d, vLLM, and KServe) for distributed inference.
* **Publish for Self-Service**: Publish models to the OpenShift AI `ModelsAsService` catalog, complete with subscription-based access permissions.
* **Enforce Governance**: Implement subscription service limits and rate limiting using Kuadrant.
* **Monitor and Report**: Generate chargeback reports and track GPU utilization metrics using Grafana.
* **Integrate Seamlessly**: Connect AI models into applications using OpenAI-compatible APIs with robust retry logic.
