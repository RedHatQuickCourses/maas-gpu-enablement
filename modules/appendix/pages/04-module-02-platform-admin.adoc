== MaaS encompasses the following key capabilities:

Tier-based access control::
Defining multiple service tiers, such as free, premium, or enterprise, each featuring distinct rate limits, quotas, and model access permissions tailored for various user groups.
Policy and quota management::
Enforcing rate limiting and quota policies to mitigate the risk of resource exhaustion.
API key authentication::
Regulating access to models through tier-based resource allocation and API key-based authentication.
Usage tracking::
Monitoring consumption metrics essential for cost allocation and billing processes.
Observability::
Providing the ability to monitor model access patterns, policy enforcement actions, and resource utilization.


== Tiers

*Tiers control which AI asset model endpoints users can access based on their group membership.*

When multiple teams share large language models, tiers serve several critical functions:

  * Preventing resource exhaustion resulting from unrestricted usage.
  * Providing differential service levels to distinct user groups.
  * Tracking and allocating costs based on consumption by each team.
  * Controlling which teams are granted access to computationally expensive or sensitive models.

[WARNING]
MaaS assigns users to tiers based on their OpenShift group membership. In instances where a user is a member of multiple groups, the system defaults to assigning the tier with the highest level number.  Users are restricted to accessing only those models made available to their assigned tier and are precluded from accessing models in lower-level tiers, even if they belong to groups associated with those lower tiers

=== Why tiers matter for MaaS

Tier-based governance provides several critical capabilities:

* *Fair resource allocation*: Prevents Free tier users from consuming resources needed by Enterprise tier production workloads
* *Cost control*: Rate limits and quotas cap resource consumption per tier
* *Chargeback reporting*: Track and allocate AI infrastructure costs by tier and business unit
* *Quality of service*: Enterprise tier gets higher priority during peak demand

Without tiers, the first team to use the platform could monopolize all available GPU resources, blocking other business units.
