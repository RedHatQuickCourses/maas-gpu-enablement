2.3: Defining Subscriptions and Token Limits

We have identified our users (Groups). Now, it is time to build the subscription plans that will govern exactly how much compute those users can consume.

In OpenShift AI 3.4, governance is centralized. Instead of deploying policies into your individual model namespaces, all subscriptions and authorization policies must be deployed into the central models-as-a-service namespace. This allows platform administrators to manage billing and quotas globally.

The ChocoTech Subscription Strategy

Based on the CIO’s requirements, we will implement the following structure:

Subscription Priority Level Token Limits (Llama 3) Enterprise Value

Gold Plan (Quality Control)

100 (Highest)

50,000 tokens/min

Guaranteed Production: Ensures the factory floor defect-detection models are never throttled during peak demand.

Silver Plan (R&D)

50 (Medium)

10,000 tokens/min

Balanced Development: Provides generous capacity for recipe optimization testing without threatening Gold tier resources.

Bronze Plan (Marketing)

10 (Lowest)

2,000 tokens/min

Cost Control: Strictly limits exploratory sentiment analysis, mathematically preventing runaway scripts from wasting expensive GPU cycles.

Exercise 1: Deploy the Subscriptions

We will use GitOps (Infrastructure-as-Code) to define the business logic for these three plans.

Notice the modelRefs section in the YAML below. Even though we will not deploy the llama-3-8b model until Module 3, MaaS allows us to "pre-wire" our governance. We are explicitly telling the subscription: "When a model reference named llama-3-8b-ref appears in the chocotech-models namespace, apply these limits."

Execute the following block to deploy all three MaaSSubscription policies into the centralized models-as-a-service namespace:

cat <<EOF | oc apply -f -
---
apiVersion: maas.opendatahub.io/v1alpha1
kind: MaaSSubscription
metadata:
  name: gold-plan
  namespace: models-as-a-service
spec:
  priority: 100
  owner:
    groups:
      - name: chocotech-quality-control
    users: []
  modelRefs:
    - name: llama-3-8b-ref
      namespace: chocotech-models
      tokenRateLimits:
        - limit: 50000
          window: 1m
---
apiVersion: maas.opendatahub.io/v1alpha1
kind: MaaSSubscription
metadata:
  name: silver-plan
  namespace: models-as-a-service
spec:
  priority: 50
  owner:
    groups:
      - name: chocotech-rd
    users: []
  modelRefs:
    - name: llama-3-8b-ref
      namespace: chocotech-models
      tokenRateLimits:
        - limit: 10000
          window: 1m
---
apiVersion: maas.opendatahub.io/v1alpha1
kind: MaaSSubscription
metadata:
  name: bronze-plan
  namespace: models-as-a-service
spec:
  priority: 10
  owner:
    groups:
      - name: chocotech-marketing
    users: []
  modelRefs:
    - name: llama-3-8b-ref
      namespace: chocotech-models
      tokenRateLimits:
        - limit: 2000
          window: 1m
EOF

Expected output: The terminal will confirm the creation of gold-plan, silver-plan, and bronze-plan.

Verification

Verify that the MaaS operator successfully registered the subscriptions in the central namespace:

oc get maassubscription -n models-as-a-service

Expected output:

NAME          PRIORITY   AGE
bronze-plan   10         30s
gold-plan     100        30s
silver-plan   50         30s

You have successfully defined what the groups are allowed to do. However, as we learned in the "Two-Part Access Rule," a subscription alone does not open the door. If a developer attempts to hit the API Gateway right now, their request will be blocked.

In the next section, we will deploy the MaaSAuthPolicy to physically authorize this traffic at the network edge.
