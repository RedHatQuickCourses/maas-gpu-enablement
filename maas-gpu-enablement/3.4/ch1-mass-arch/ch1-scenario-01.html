Course Scenario: ChocoTech Industries

Your Role and Mission

In this course, you will take on the role of a Platform Engineer at ChocoTech Industries, a modern chocolate manufacturing company. You have been assigned to solve a critical infrastructure challenge that is actively hindering multiple business units.

ChocoTech has teams across Quality Control, R&D, Marketing, and Supply Chain, all trying to leverage AI for their operations. However, each team has purchased and siloed their own GPU infrastructure, leading to a company-wide "GPU Silo Crisis."

  • Your Assignment: Design and implement a Models as a Service (MaaS) platform on Red Hat OpenShift AI to transform fragmented GPU resources into a centralized, governed model hub.

  • The Objective: Enable all lines of business to access generative AI models through a self-service catalog with proper governance. This will eliminate resource waste and accelerate business innovation.

The Problem: ChocoTech’s GPU Silo Crisis

ChocoTech has invested heavily in AI, but their decentralized approach has created serious operational and financial bottlenecks.

The Current State

Four teams independently purchased and deployed their own GPU hardware:

  • Quality Control: Running computer vision models for defect detection (2x A100 GPUs).

  • R&D: Testing recipe optimization and flavor prediction models (4x L40S GPUs).

  • Marketing: Analyzing customer sentiment and demand forecasting (2x L4 GPUs).

  • Supply Chain: Running inventory prediction models (2x L40S GPUs).

Operational Pain Points

  • Severe Under-Utilization: Quality Control’s GPUs sit idle 60% of the time while R&D waits weeks for capacity, prompting unnecessary hardware purchases exceeding $200,000.

  • Misallocated Expertise: Data scientists spend 40% of their time debugging CUDA drivers, Docker containers, and Kubernetes manifests instead of tuning models and analyzing data.

  • Redundant Deployments: All four teams independently downloaded and deployed the Llama-3 8B model, wasting 32GB of storage and compute capacity per deployment.

  • Security "Black Boxes": Without a centralized API gateway, there is zero visibility into who is sending what data to which model, making audit logs and PII filtering impossible to enforce.

  • No Chargeback Visibility: Finance cannot justify infrastructure costs without clear visibility into model usage across users and applications.

The MaaS Opportunity We need to transition from hardware management to token delivery. MaaS transforms GPU infrastructure from dedicated, single-team clusters into a consumable backplane. Centralized IT manages the hardware, inference strategy, and governance policies (via KServe, Kuadrant, and the Gateway API), while the lines of business simply consume AI capabilities via tokens.

Your Vision of Success

If your MaaS implementation succeeds, here is the impact you will deliver to ChocoTech:

Immediate Improvements (Short-Term)

  • Eliminated Redundancy: A single Llama-3 8B deployment serves all four teams, resulting in a 75% reduction in storage and compute waste.

  • Fair Resource Allocation: Tier-based quotas prevent Quality Control from monopolizing GPUs while R&D waits, ensuring balanced access.

  • Complete Audit Trail: Every API request is logged with user identity, the model accessed, and tokens consumed to satisfy compliance requirements.

  • Simplified Developer Experience: The Marketing team can integrate models via an OpenAI-compatible API in hours instead of weeks.

Strategic Benefits (Long-Term)

  • Accurate Chargebacks: Token-based consumption tracking enables Finance to bill departments based on actual usage, justifying infrastructure investments.

  • Centralized Catalog Governance: The platform team controls which models are available, preventing "Shadow AI" and standardizing security.

  • Maximized GPU Efficiency: Intelligent routing and centralized inference reduce overall infrastructure costs by 40-50%.

  • Accelerated Innovation: Developers focus on building applications instead of managing infrastructure, vastly increasing team productivity.

Mission Success Criteria

By the end of this QuickCourse, you will have practical MaaS implementation skills to address ChocoTech’s challenges. You will be able to:

  • Prevent "Shadow AI": Understand the business impact of the GPU silo crisis and eliminate redundant deployments.

  • Establish Centralized Serving: Configure the MaaS platform through OpenShift AI operator Custom Resource Definitions (CRDs).

  • Enable Efficient Utilization: Deploy models using distributed inference with llm-d and vLLM across multiple accelerators.

  • Ensure Fair Allocation: Implement governance tiers with rate limiting and quotas using Kuadrant.

  • Provide Visibility: Generate usage metrics and chargeback reports to justify infrastructure costs.

  • Simplify Consumption: Integrate models into applications via OpenAI-compatible APIs so developers can skip infrastructure complexity.

Common Questions

"Can we integrate MaaS with our existing AI models without major changes?"

Yes. Module 3 demonstrates deploying existing models from Hugging Face and custom model repositories using the llm-d and vLLM serving runtimes.

"What about security and compliance considerations?"

Module 4 covers governance policies, including rate limiting, tier-based access control, and audit logging through the Kuadrant policy layer.

"How does this integrate with our current application development workflows?"

Module 5 shows OpenAI-compatible API integration that works natively with existing LangChain, LlamaIndex, and custom application frameworks.

"Is MaaS adoption sustainable for our team with limited Kubernetes expertise?"

Absolutely. This QuickCourse demonstrates operator-based configuration through OpenShift AI CRDs, minimizing low-level Kubernetes knowledge requirements while providing self-service capabilities via the dashboard.
